A Day I Will Never Forget

Clayton and Brittany Cook were posing for their wedding photos in on a park bridge in Cambridge, Ontario, when the groom noticed a young boy was struggling in the nearby water, Canada’s CTV News reports. "I look over and I see Clay in the water and I think, what is he doing?" the bride told CTV Kitchener. Within seconds of noticing there was a child in danger, her husband had jumped into the river - in his wedding suit - to pull him on to dry land. “His face was underwater and he was fighting, like he was really fighting,” Clayton Cook said. “Luckily, he was only a little guy…and I honestly just sort of hopped him up.”
To the couple and the wedding photographer, who captured the groom's heroic moment, it looked as though another child had pushed the boy in. Thankfully, he was okay and was taken home by an older sibling.
Following the dramatic photo session, photographer Darren Hatt shared two pictures of the unexpected rescue mission on his Facebook page. Many commenters have described Cook's efforts as "unbelievable" and "courageous". “It was a commendable thing that he did and he sprung into action incredibly quick,” Hatt said. “Almost as soon as I realised what was going on, he had already saved the day.”
As for Mrs Cook, she said her husband’s selfless actions are exactly why she married him. “That's Clay, like that's Clay to me,” she said. “It's something he would just instinctively do. It was like fate almost, we were meant to be there." It might have been a dramatic start to married life, but it sounds like these two are made for each other
